When you order a used auto part on AUAPW, your part ships directly from the verified salvage yard that listed it. Each seller is responsible for packaging and dispatching your order, but AUAPW sets the standards they must follow — so you get your part safely, on time, and exactly as described.

## Shipping Carriers

AUAPW sellers use a range of trusted carriers depending on the size and weight of your part.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="UPS" icon="box">
    Used for most standard-sized parts including body panels, interior trim, sensors, pumps, and other small-to-medium components.
  </Card>
  <Card title="FedEx" icon="truck">
    Available for standard and expedited shipments on parts that meet FedEx dimensional and weight limits.
  </Card>
  <Card title="USPS" icon="envelope">
    Typically used for small, lightweight parts such as clips, switches, emblems, and other accessories.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Freight (LTL)" icon="warehouse">
    Required for heavy assemblies — engines, transmissions, differentials, axle assemblies, and full doors. Less-Than-Truckload (LTL) freight includes liftgate service for residential delivery.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Shipping Options

You'll see the available shipping options for each part on the product listing page and again at checkout. Availability depends on part size, seller location, and your delivery address.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Standard Shipping" icon="clock">
    **5–7 business days.** The most affordable option for non-urgent orders. Available for the vast majority of parts on AUAPW.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Expedited Shipping" icon="bolt">
    **2–3 business days.** Faster delivery when you need your part soon. Available on most small-to-medium parts from participating sellers.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Overnight Shipping" icon="circle-arrow-up">
    **Next business day.** Available where offered by the seller and carrier. Not all parts or locations qualify. Order early in the day to maximize eligibility.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Freight Shipping" icon="truck-moving">
    **7–14 business days.** Required for engines, transmissions, and large assemblies. Includes liftgate service so the carrier can lower the pallet to ground level at your address.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  Shipping to **Hawaii, Alaska, and Puerto Rico** may have limited carrier availability. Large or heavy parts that require freight shipping to these locations often incur additional surcharges, and some sellers may not ship there at all. Check the listing's shipping destinations before purchasing.
</Note>

## How Shipping Costs Are Calculated

Your shipping cost is calculated automatically at checkout based on four key factors:

1. **Part weight and dimensions** — Heavier and bulkier parts cost more to ship. Freight shipments are priced by freight class, which factors in density.
2. **Seller location** — The distance between the seller's salvage yard and your delivery address directly affects the carrier's rate.
3. **Your delivery address** — Residential addresses may incur a carrier surcharge compared to commercial addresses. Remote or rural ZIP codes can also affect pricing.
4. **Chosen shipping method** — Expedited and overnight options carry a premium over standard rates. Freight pricing is quoted separately based on the shipment specifics.

<Tip>
  If you need multiple parts, check whether they're listed by the same seller. Combining parts from the same seller into a single order often qualifies you for a combined shipping discount — saving you money compared to placing separate orders.
</Tip>

## Packaging Requirements

AUAPW requires all sellers to meet minimum packaging standards before dispatching your order. Here's what those standards include:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Fluid Drainage">
    Sellers must drain all fluids from mechanical parts — including engines, transmissions, differentials, and power steering components — before packaging. This is required by carrier regulations and prevents leakage in transit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Protective Wrapping">
    Parts must be wrapped in bubble wrap, foam padding, or similar protective material to prevent damage during handling. Fragile components such as lights, mirrors, and glass panels receive additional cushioning.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Secure Boxing">
    Standard parts ship in sturdy corrugated boxes sized appropriately for the part. Sellers may not reuse damaged or compromised packaging.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pallet and Crating for Freight">
    Engines, transmissions, and other large assemblies must be mounted on a pallet and secured with strapping and shrink wrap. Some sellers crate high-value assemblies for added protection.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  If your part arrives and the packaging shows visible damage, document it with photos before opening. This protects your ability to file a damage claim if the part itself is affected. See our [Returns Policy](/shipping/returns-policy) for next steps.
</Info>
