Your delivery date on AUAPW is the sum of two separate timeframes: the seller's handling time and the carrier's transit time. Understanding how both work together helps you set accurate expectations and place your order with enough lead time to get your vehicle back on the road.

## Handling Time

Handling time is the period between when you place your order and when the seller hands the package off to the carrier. On AUAPW, the standard handling window is **1–2 business days**. During this time, the seller pulls the part from inventory, inspects it, drains any fluids if required, packages it to AUAPW standards, and generates a shipping label.

Some high-volume sellers offer **same-day handling**, meaning they'll dispatch your order on the same business day if you place it before their daily cutoff time (typically noon in their local time zone). You'll see a same-day handling badge on qualifying listings.

<Tip>
  Place your order early in the morning to maximize your chances of same-day dispatch. Orders placed after a seller's cutoff time will typically ship the following business day, adding a full day to your estimated delivery.
</Tip>

## Transit Times by Shipping Method

Once the carrier picks up your part, transit time depends on the shipping method you selected at checkout. The times below reflect business days after the seller dispatches your order.

| Shipping Method | Transit Time After Dispatch | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard** | 3–5 business days | Non-urgent orders, most part types |
| **Expedited** | 1–2 business days | Time-sensitive repairs, small-to-medium parts |
| **Overnight** | Next business day | Emergency repairs, where available |
| **Freight (LTL)** | 5–10 business days | Engines, transmissions, large assemblies |

<Info>
  Transit times are estimates provided by the carrier and are not guaranteed delivery dates. Remote or rural delivery addresses, residential surcharge zones, and multi-hub routing can add one or more business days to the estimate.
</Info>

## Estimated Delivery Date

The estimated delivery date you see on the checkout page and in your order confirmation email is calculated as:

**Estimated Delivery = Order Date + Handling Time + Transit Time**

For example, if you order on a Monday, the seller has a 1-business-day handling time, and you select Standard shipping (3–5 business days transit), your estimated delivery window would be Thursday through the following Monday.

AUAPW recalculates your estimated delivery date in real time once the seller generates a tracking number and the carrier scans the package. You'll receive an updated delivery estimate by email as soon as tracking is active. Check your order status at any time by visiting **My Orders** in your account dashboard.

<Note>
  **Freight deliveries require a delivery appointment.** The freight carrier will call the phone number on your order to schedule a delivery window — typically a 4-hour window on your chosen date. Make sure your contact number is current in your account settings, and plan to be present to receive the shipment.
</Note>

## Delays

Delays can happen for reasons outside the seller's or AUAPW's control. The most common causes include:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="High-Demand Periods" icon="calendar">
    Carrier networks experience congestion around major holidays and during peak retail seasons. Build in extra time for orders placed in late November and December.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Weather Events" icon="cloud-bolt">
    Severe weather — including winter storms, hurricanes, and flooding — can suspend carrier operations in affected regions and cause multi-day delays.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Carrier Exceptions" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    Missed scans, damaged labels, and misdirected packages can create carrier exceptions. These usually self-correct within 1–2 days as the carrier re-routes your package.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Freight Scheduling" icon="phone">
    Freight deliveries depend on your availability for an appointment. If the carrier cannot reach you, delivery may be rescheduled, extending your wait.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

**If your order is delayed**, here's what to do:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check Your Tracking">
    Open your order in **My Orders** and click the tracking number to view the latest carrier scan. Most delays are visible in the tracking history.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait One Business Day">
    Many carrier exceptions resolve automatically. If the tracking hasn't updated in more than 48 hours, proceed to the next step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Contact the Seller">
    Send the seller a message directly through your order page. They can open a trace with the carrier on your behalf.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open a Support Case">
    If the seller doesn't respond within 2 business days or the package appears lost, open a support case with AUAPW. Our team will investigate and, if necessary, initiate a claim with the carrier.
  </Step>
</Steps>
