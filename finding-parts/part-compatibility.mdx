Ordering the wrong part wastes time, costs return shipping, and can delay a critical repair. AUAPW's compatibility system automatically cross-references every listing against your saved vehicle profile — so you see a clear signal on each listing page before you ever click **Add to Cart**. Understanding how that system works, and when to verify manually, helps you buy with confidence every time.

## How Compatibility Is Checked

AUAPW uses two complementary methods to determine whether a listed part fits your vehicle:

- **OEM Part Number Cross-Referencing** — Each listing is tagged with the original equipment manufacturer (OEM) part number stamped on the part when it left the factory. AUAPW's database maps those numbers against the OEM fitment specifications for millions of year-make-model-trim combinations, flagging parts that share the same number or a confirmed interchange equivalent.
- **Vehicle Fitment Databases** — AUAPW partners with industry-standard automotive fitment data providers to maintain an up-to-date catalogue of which parts interchange across vehicles. When you search with your vehicle on file, every result is filtered through this database in real time.

Together, these two layers catch the vast majority of fitment mismatches automatically — including parts that look visually identical but differ in mounting points, connector types, or calibration.

## When to Double-Check

Even a green badge warrants a quick manual review in the following situations:

- **Different Trim Levels** — Two trims of the same model year can use entirely different suspension, brake, or electrical components. Confirm your trim is an exact match to what the listing specifies.
- **Factory Option Packages** — Features like a towing package, sport package, or premium audio system often require unique parts that differ from the base-model equivalent.
- **Regional Variants** — Vehicles sold in different markets (e.g., North America vs. Europe) sometimes carry different part numbers for the same position, even under the same nameplate.
- **Aftermarket Modifications** — If your vehicle has been lowered, upgraded, or otherwise modified from factory spec, compatibility data based on stock configurations may not apply.

<Warning>
  Trim-level differences are one of the most common causes of fitment problems. For example, a sport-trim suspension strut is typically not interchangeable with a base-trim strut — even on the same model year — because spring rates, mount geometry, and electronic damping connectors can all differ. Always confirm your exact trim code before ordering suspension, braking, or drivetrain components.
</Warning>

## Manually Verifying Compatibility

When you want to go beyond the badge and confirm fitment yourself, follow these steps:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check the OEM Part Number on the Listing">
    Find the **OEM Part Number** field in the Part Details section of the listing. This number is the definitive factory identifier for the part.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Compare with Your Vehicle's Specifications">
    Look up your vehicle's OEM part number for the same position using your owner's manual, a dealership parts lookup, or a trusted parts catalogue. If the numbers match — or if a known interchange relationship exists — the part fits.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Contact the Seller if You're Still Unsure">
    Use the **Message Seller** button on the listing page to ask the seller for the year, make, model, trim, and mileage of the donor vehicle the part was pulled from. A reputable seller will respond promptly and accurately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Compatibility Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is an OEM part number?">
    An OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) part number is the unique identifier assigned by the vehicle manufacturer to every component it installs at the factory. It encodes the part's design specification, so two parts with the same OEM number are functionally and dimensionally identical. You can find your vehicle's OEM part numbers in the owner's manual, a dealership's parts department, or an authoritative online parts catalogue.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="My vehicle has aftermarket modifications — will parts fit?">
    Possibly, but you need to verify manually. AUAPW's compatibility system is built around factory (stock) configurations. If your vehicle has been lowered, has an aftermarket engine swap, uses non-stock wheel sizes, or has been otherwise modified, the stock OEM fitment data may not reflect your actual setup. Contact the seller, describe your modifications, and confirm measurements or connector types before purchasing.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I search by part number directly?">
    Yes. On the AUAPW search page, select the **Part Number** tab and enter the full OEM part number or a known interchange number. The search will return all listings tagged with that number or a confirmed equivalent. This is the fastest route if you already know the exact part number you need.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
