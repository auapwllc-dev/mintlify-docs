Every AUAPW part listing is a snapshot of a physical component sitting on a shelf at a verified salvage yard. Knowing how to read each section of that listing — from the condition grade and seller rating to the interchange numbers and warranty terms — gives you everything you need to evaluate the part before committing to a purchase. This page walks you through each section in the order it appears on the listing page.

## Part Condition Grades

The condition grade is the first thing to look for. AUAPW uses a standardized grading scale so you can compare parts across different sellers on equal terms.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Grade A — Excellent" icon="star" href="finding-parts/reading-listings">
    Low mileage, minimal visible wear, and fully functional. Grade A parts typically come from late-model donor vehicles involved in collision damage, where the mechanical components were unaffected. Ideal when you want near-new quality at a used price.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Grade B — Good" icon="thumbs-up" href="finding-parts/reading-listings">
    Normal wear consistent with the part's mileage, fully functional with no defects affecting operation. The most common grade on AUAPW and a solid choice for most repairs.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Grade C — Fair" icon="circle-check" href="finding-parts/reading-listings">
    Visible wear or minor cosmetic damage, but mechanically sound and fully operational. Grade C parts are a budget-friendly option for non-visible components or vehicles where cosmetics are not a priority.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Remanufactured" icon="arrows-rotate" href="finding-parts/reading-listings">
    Rebuilt to OEM specifications using a combination of original and new sub-components. Remanufactured parts undergo performance testing before listing and often carry longer warranty coverage than used grades.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  For high-wear items like alternators, starters, and brake calipers, a **Remanufactured** part often delivers better long-term value than a Grade B or C used part — the rebuild process replaces the components most likely to fail, and warranty coverage is typically stronger.
</Tip>

## Seller Information

The seller card appears near the top of every listing and tells you who you're buying from.

- **Star Rating** — Sellers are rated 1–5 stars based on buyer reviews covering accuracy of description, packaging quality, and shipping speed. Prioritize sellers with 4 stars or higher for the best experience.
- **Years on Platform** — The number of years the salvage yard has been a verified AUAPW seller. Longer tenure generally reflects a track record of reliable transactions.
- **Fulfillment Rate** — The percentage of orders the seller has shipped on time and without cancellation. A fulfillment rate above 95% indicates a dependable operation.
- **Location** — The seller's city and state. Choosing a seller closer to your ZIP code can significantly reduce shipping time and cost, and may allow for local pickup on qualifying orders.

<Note>
  You can message a seller directly before purchasing by clicking the **Message Seller** button on the listing page. Use this to ask about the donor vehicle, request additional photos, or confirm shipping options — most sellers respond within one business day.
</Note>

## Part Details

The Part Details section contains the technical information you need to confirm you're ordering the right component.

- **Mileage on the Part** — The odometer reading of the donor vehicle at the time the part was removed. Lower mileage generally means less wear, though proper maintenance history matters equally for mechanical parts.
- **OEM Part Number** — The manufacturer's original part number stamped on or cast into the component. Use this to verify fitment against your vehicle's service documentation or a dealership parts lookup.
- **Interchange Numbers** — Alternative OEM part numbers for equivalent parts used across different vehicle years, makes, or models. If the exact part is out of stock, interchange numbers help you find functionally identical alternatives from different donor vehicles.
- **Donor Vehicle** — The specific year, make, model, and trim of the vehicle the part was pulled from. This is the most direct way to confirm fitment when the OEM number alone leaves questions.

<Tip>
  Always check the **interchange numbers** listed in the Part Details section. A part pulled from a different model or even a different manufacturer may be identical in specification to what you need — and searching by interchange number can surface lower-priced alternatives when your exact part is listed at a premium.
</Tip>

## Photos

All AUAPW listings include at least one photo of the actual part. High-quality listings include multiple angles showing connectors, mounting points, and any visible wear.

- **Browse the photo gallery** by clicking the main listing image to open the full-screen viewer. Use the arrow controls to scroll through all available photos.
- **Zoom in** on connector types, brackets, and mounting holes to compare them against your current part before ordering.
- **Request additional photos** if the listing doesn't show the angle or detail you need. Click **Message Seller**, specify which views you'd like (e.g., "connector side," "mounting flange," "part number stamp"), and the seller can upload new images directly to the listing.

<Info>
  Sellers who respond to photo requests quickly and thoroughly are demonstrating the same attention to detail you can expect throughout the transaction. A seller willing to provide extra photos before the sale is a good sign.
</Info>

## Warranty

Every part sold on AUAPW includes a baseline warranty, with optional extended coverage available at checkout.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Standard 30-Day Warranty">
    All used parts sold through AUAPW are covered by a **30-day return warranty** from the date of delivery. If the part arrives in a condition that does not match the listing description, or if it fails within 30 days under normal use, you are entitled to a full replacement or refund. File a warranty claim through **My Orders → Report an Issue** on your AUAPW account.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Extended Warranty">
    At checkout, you can add an **Extended Warranty** of 90 days or 6 months, depending on the part category. Extended warranties are underwritten by AUAPW's warranty partner and cover mechanical failure under normal operating conditions beyond the standard 30-day window. Extended warranty pricing is shown as a line item during checkout before you confirm your order.

    <Note>
      Extended warranties are available on most mechanical and electrical components — including engines, transmissions, alternators, and starters — but are not available on body panels, glass, or interior trim, which are covered only under the standard 30-day warranty for condition mismatches.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Warning>
  Warranty coverage is voided if the part shows evidence of improper installation, modification after receipt, or damage caused by a related failing component (for example, an engine installed into a vehicle with a known coolant leak). Always address root-cause issues before installing a replacement part.
</Warning>
