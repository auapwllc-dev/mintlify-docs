Whether you're just starting your application or you're a seasoned seller working through an edge case, this FAQ covers the most common questions we hear from sellers on AUAPW. If your question isn't answered here, you can reach the Seller Support team at **sellers@auapw.org** or through the **Help** link in your Seller Dashboard.

---

## Getting Started

<Accordion title="Who is eligible to sell on AUAPW?">
  AUAPW is open to **licensed businesses** that sell used auto parts professionally. Eligible seller types include:

  - Licensed salvage yards holding a current state-issued salvage or dismantler license
  - Auto recyclers registered with a state or national recycling program
  - Used auto parts dealers with a valid business license and an active physical inventory

  You must also have a federal **Employer Identification Number (EIN)** and a U.S. business bank account for payouts. Sole proprietors with an EIN and a business license are eligible. Individuals selling parts from personally owned vehicles are not permitted to sell on AUAPW.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How long does seller verification take?">
  Verification typically takes **24–48 business hours** from the time you submit a complete application with all required documents. The onboarding team reviews applications Monday through Friday, 8 AM–6 PM CT. Applications submitted over the weekend begin processing the following Monday.

  If your application requires additional documentation or clarification, you'll receive an email from **no-reply@auapw.org** with specific instructions. Responding promptly to these requests keeps your verification on track. Once approved, your account is activated immediately and you can begin listing parts.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I sell as an individual, or do I need a business?">
  You need an active business entity to sell on AUAPW. Sole proprietors are welcome, but you must have a valid business license issued by your state and an EIN (you cannot use a Social Security Number in place of an EIN). If you are operating a salvage or recycling business as a sole proprietor, register your business with your state's licensing authority before applying. Individual consumers who occasionally sell parts from vehicles they own are not eligible to register as sellers.
</Accordion>

## Listings & Inventory

<Accordion title="How many parts can I list?">
  Your listing limit depends on your seller tier:

  - **Standard:** Up to 500 active listings at any time
  - **Professional:** Unlimited active listings
  - **Enterprise:** Unlimited active listings with API access for automated inventory sync

  You can upgrade your tier at any time from **Account Settings → Seller Tier**. Upgrades take effect immediately.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens if I list a part but it's already sold in my yard?">
  If a part sells through another channel (in person, by phone, or on another platform) before a buyer orders it on AUAPW, you are responsible for updating the listing status to **Sold** or **Unavailable** as soon as possible. If a buyer places an order for a part you no longer have, you must cancel the order — which negatively impacts your fulfillment rate and seller rating.

  To avoid this, build a habit of updating your AUAPW listings every time a part leaves your inventory through any channel. Professional and Enterprise sellers can use the CSV bulk upload to push status updates for multiple parts at once.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I list parts without photos?">
  No. Every listing on AUAPW requires a minimum of **3 photos**. Listings submitted with fewer than 3 photos will not publish. This policy exists because listings with photos convert at dramatically higher rates than those without, and buyer disputes related to part condition drop significantly when clear, accurate photos are provided.

  Photos must be at least 800×600 pixels, must show the actual part (no stock images), and should include a clear shot of the OEM part number tag. See [How to List Parts](/sellers/listing-parts) for full photo requirements and tips.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What OEM numbers do I need?">
  At minimum, you need the **OEM part number** — the number stamped, printed, or tagged directly on the part by the original manufacturer. This is the number buyers use to confirm they're ordering the right part.

  In addition, we strongly recommend entering the **interchange number** from a cross-reference catalog (such as Hollander or MOTOR). Interchange numbers allow your listing to surface in searches from buyers who are searching across multiple compatible applications, not just the exact OEM number. If you use a parts management system like Car-Part Pro or Hollander, interchange numbers are already in your data and easy to copy over.
</Accordion>

## Orders & Fulfillment

<Accordion title="What happens when I receive an order?">
  When a buyer places an order, you receive an email notification and a dashboard alert under **Orders → Pending Shipment**. The order will include the buyer's shipping address, the part ordered, and the order number.

  You have **2 business days** to ship the order. Generate your shipping label from the order detail page, package the part according to AUAPW's packaging standards, and hand it off to the carrier. Once the carrier scans the package, tracking updates automatically and the order status moves to "Shipped." The buyer is notified by email with tracking information.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if I can't fulfill an order after it's placed?">
  If you cannot fulfill an order — because the part is no longer available, was damaged on removal, or was listed in error — you must cancel the order as quickly as possible. To cancel:

  1. Go to **Orders → Pending Shipment**
  2. Open the order and click **Cancel Order**
  3. Select the cancellation reason and submit

  The buyer is automatically refunded in full. Cancellations due to inventory errors count against your fulfillment rate. Multiple cancellations may trigger a performance review. The best way to prevent this is to mark parts as sold or unavailable as soon as they leave your inventory.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I handle a buyer claiming a part doesn't fit?">
  A fitment claim means the buyer believes the part you sold does not fit their vehicle as described in your listing. Here's how to respond:

  1. Review your listing to confirm the donor vehicle year/make/model/trim you listed and verify the buyer's vehicle against standard interchange data.
  2. If your listing was accurate and the buyer ordered the wrong part, respond through the **Orders → Messages** tool with documentation (your listing details, VIN, interchange data) supporting the fitment.
  3. If your listing contained an error (wrong year, wrong trim), accept the return and work with the buyer to resolve it promptly.

  AUAPW's dispute team reviews fitment claims using the VIN and OEM data from your listing. Listings with complete VIN and interchange number data resolve faster and more often in the seller's favor.
</Accordion>

## Fees & Payouts

<Accordion title="What fees does AUAPW charge?">
  AUAPW charges three types of fees:

  - **Listing fee:** Free for your first 100 listings per month. $0.10 per listing for each listing beyond 100 in a given month.
  - **Transaction fee:** 8% of the item sale price (not including shipping).
  - **Payment processing fee:** 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction, applied to the total order amount including shipping.

  Standard tier sellers pay no monthly subscription. Professional and Enterprise tiers carry a monthly fee that is disclosed at signup and visible in **Account Settings → Billing**. There are no setup fees, photo hosting fees, or inactivity fees.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How often are payouts processed?">
  Payouts are processed **every Tuesday** via ACH bank transfer for all orders that reached "delivered" status during the prior Monday–Sunday period. ACH deposits typically arrive in your bank account within 1–2 business days after processing (Wednesday or Thursday of the same week).

  The minimum payout threshold is **$25.00**. If your available balance is below $25 at the time of processing, it rolls over to the following week's payout. You can view your pending balance and payout history at any time under **Dashboard → Payouts**.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens to my payout if a return is approved?">
  When a return is approved, the refund amount is deducted from your next scheduled payout. The deduction includes:

  - The item sale price (refunded to the buyer)
  - The AUAPW transaction fee (8%) — **not refunded to you** if the return was due to a listing error (wrong grade, wrong part, or fitment inaccuracy you caused)
  - The payment processing fee (2.9% + $0.30) — **not refunded** in any case, as this fee is charged by the payment processor

  If the return is due to buyer error (ordered the wrong part, changed their mind), you retain the transaction fee and the buyer pays a restocking fee. In this case, you are only responsible for the cost of the return shipping label if you choose to provide one; AUAPW may require the buyer to cover return shipping for buyer-error returns.
</Accordion>

## Returns & Disputes

<Accordion title="How do seller returns work?">
  Buyers have **30 days from the delivery date** to initiate a return request. To request a return, a buyer submits a reason through their order history. You will be notified via email and dashboard alert.

  **Valid return reasons include:**
  - Part does not match the listing description (wrong grade, wrong part number, incorrect fitment information)
  - Part arrived damaged due to packaging failure
  - Part is non-functional in a way not disclosed in the listing

  **Non-valid return reasons** (buyer error):
  - Buyer ordered the wrong part for their vehicle
  - Buyer changed their mind after the part arrived
  - Part does not fit due to a vehicle modification not related to the listed fitment

  You have **48 hours** to respond to a return request before AUAPW's dispute team steps in. You can accept the return, offer a partial refund, or dispute the claim with documentation.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I dispute a return request?">
  Yes. If you believe a return request is invalid — for example, the buyer ordered the wrong part or is misrepresenting the condition — you can dispute the request within **48 hours** of receiving it.

  To dispute a return:
  1. Go to **Orders → Returns** and open the return request
  2. Click **Dispute Return**
  3. Enter your response and upload supporting evidence (photos, listing screenshots, VIN records, interchange documentation)
  4. Submit the dispute

  AUAPW's dispute resolution team will review both sides and issue a decision within **5 business days**. During this period, no refund is processed and no payout deduction occurs.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What is AUAPW's dispute resolution process?">
  When a return dispute is submitted, AUAPW's Seller Trust team reviews all available evidence:

  - Your original listing (photos, description, condition grade, VIN, OEM number)
  - The buyer's return reason and any photos they submitted
  - Carrier tracking and delivery records
  - Interchange and fitment data from third-party databases

  The team will contact both parties if additional information is needed. A final decision is issued within **5 business days** and is communicated by email. Decisions are binding under the AUAPW Seller Agreement, though sellers may escalate to a second review within 7 days of the decision by emailing **disputes@auapw.org** with new evidence not previously submitted.

  Maintaining detailed listing records — especially VIN data, accurate photos, and correct OEM numbers — is the most effective way to win disputes that are filed in error.
</Accordion>
