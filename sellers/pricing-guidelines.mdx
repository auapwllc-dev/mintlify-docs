Pricing your used auto parts competitively is one of the most direct levers you have over your sales volume on AUAPW. Set prices too high and buyers skip your listing in favor of lower-cost alternatives; set them too low and you leave money on the table and undercut your own margins. This guide walks you through AUAPW's fee structure, proven pricing strategies, and the payout process so you always know exactly what you'll net from each sale.

## AUAPW Fee Structure

AUAPW keeps its fee structure transparent and straightforward. There are no hidden charges, no monthly subscription fees for Standard sellers, and no setup costs.

| Fee Type | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Listing Fee** | Free for first 100 listings/month | $0.10 per listing after the first 100 |
| **Transaction Fee** | 8% of sale price | Applied to the item price only, not shipping |
| **Payment Processing** | 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction | Applied to the total order amount including shipping |
| **Monthly Subscription** | $0 (Standard tier) | Professional and Enterprise tiers have a monthly fee — see account settings |

**Example calculation for a $150 part sale with $20 shipping:**

- Sale price: $150.00
- Transaction fee (8%): −$12.00
- Payment processing (2.9% × $170 + $0.30): −$5.23
- **Net to seller: $132.77**

<Note>
All prices on AUAPW must be listed in **U.S. dollars (USD)**. Listings with non-USD pricing will fail to publish. International sellers (if applicable in future expansions) will use the same USD pricing system; currency conversion is handled on the buyer side.
</Note>

## Setting Competitive Prices

Before you set a price, spend a few minutes researching the market on AUAPW itself. Search for the same part number or a similar part from the same year range and review the active listings. This gives you an immediate benchmark.

**Tips for competitive pricing:**

- **Check market comparables within AUAPW.** Use the search tools a buyer would use — enter the part name, OEM number, or year/make/model and look at what competing sellers are charging for the same grade.
- **Factor in the donor vehicle's mileage.** A Grade A alternator from a 30,000-mile vehicle commands a meaningfully higher price than the same part from a 120,000-mile vehicle, even if both are functionally sound. Disclose mileage and price accordingly.
- **Account for your shipping costs.** You set the item price; buyers pay shipping separately at checkout based on the dimensions and weight you entered. Make sure your item price covers your packaging costs and handling time, but avoid building shipping costs into the part price — buyers comparison-shop total cost.
- **Review prices periodically.** Parts that have been listed for more than 30 days without a sale are often slightly overpriced relative to the current market. A 10–15% price reduction frequently generates a sale within days.

## Pricing by Condition

Condition grade is the strongest predictor of a buyer's willingness to pay. Use these general benchmarks as a starting point:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Grade A" icon="circle-check">
    **Premium pricing.** Grade A parts from low-mileage, late-model vehicles can approach 60–80% of the new OEM price, especially for high-demand categories like electronics, sensors, and interior trim.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Grade B" icon="circle-half-stroke">
    **Market-rate pricing.** Grade B parts typically sell at 35–55% of new OEM price. These are the bread-and-butter listings on AUAPW — set a competitive price and they move steadily.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Grade C" icon="circle-xmark">
    **Value pricing.** Grade C parts should generally be priced **40–60% below the prevailing market price** for a Grade B equivalent. Buyers choosing Grade C are maximizing value, so price aggressively.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Remanufactured" icon="arrows-rotate">
    **Near-new pricing.** Professionally remanufactured parts with documentation can be priced at 50–90% of new OEM price, depending on the category and the remanufacturer's reputation.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Promotions

Professional and Enterprise sellers can run time-limited promotions directly from the Seller Dashboard under **Inventory → Promotions**.

**Available promotion types:**
- **Percentage discount** — apply a discount (e.g., 15% off) to a single listing, a category, or your entire catalog for a defined date range
- **Fixed-amount discount** — reduce the price by a set dollar amount
- **Bundle pricing** — offer a reduced price when a buyer purchases multiple parts from the same order (Enterprise only)

Promoted listings display a sale badge in search results, which increases click-through rates. Promotions are optional and do not affect listing fees or transaction fee rates.

<Info>
Standard tier sellers cannot run promotions through the dashboard but can manually reduce prices on individual listings at any time. Upgrading to Professional unlocks the promotions tool along with priority support and unlimited listings.
</Info>

## Payouts

AUAPW pays sellers via **weekly ACH bank deposit** every Tuesday for all completed orders from the prior week (Monday through Sunday).

**Payout rules:**
- Minimum payout threshold: **$25.00** — balances below $25 roll over to the next week
- Payments are deposited to the bank account you connected during registration
- To update your bank account, go to **Account Settings → Payouts**
- A payout summary with itemized transactions is emailed to you each Tuesday and available in your dashboard under **Payouts → History**

<Accordion title="When do I receive my payout?">
  Payouts are processed every **Tuesday** via ACH bank transfer and typically arrive in your account within 1–2 business days (Wednesday or Thursday). The payout covers all orders that reached "delivered" status during the previous Monday–Sunday period. Orders disputed or under review at the time of processing are held until the dispute is resolved.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens if a buyer returns a part?">
  If a return is approved, the transaction amount — minus the payment processing fee, which is non-refundable — is deducted from your next payout. The AUAPW transaction fee (8%) is refunded to the buyer but is not refunded to you in cases where the return is due to an inaccurate listing (wrong grade, wrong part, fitment error). Returns due to buyer error (ordered wrong part, changed mind) are subject to a restocking fee paid by the buyer, and you retain the transaction fee. See the [Seller FAQ](/sellers/seller-faq) for full return details.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Are there any hidden fees?">
  No. AUAPW charges only the fees listed in the table above: a listing fee for listings beyond 100/month, an 8% transaction fee, and a 2.9% + $0.30 payment processing fee. There are no account setup fees, photo hosting fees, search placement fees, or inactivity fees. Professional and Enterprise tiers carry a monthly subscription, but that cost is disclosed up front in your tier selection and billing settings.
</Accordion>
