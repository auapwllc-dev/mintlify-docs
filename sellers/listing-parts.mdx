Every listing you publish on AUAPW is indexed by part name, OEM number, interchange number, and vehicle fitment — so the more complete your listing, the more buyers can find it. Taking a few extra minutes to fill in all available fields, upload quality photos, and assign the correct condition grade pays off in faster sales, fewer buyer questions, and stronger seller ratings.

## Creating a Listing

Use the following steps to publish a single part listing from your Seller Dashboard. If you have a large catalog to upload at once, see [Managing Inventory](/sellers/managing-inventory) for bulk upload instructions.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log In to Your Seller Dashboard">
    Go to [auapw.org/dashboard](https://auapw.org/dashboard) and sign in with your seller credentials. All listing management tools are accessible from the left-hand navigation under **Inventory**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click 'Add New Part'">
    Select **Inventory → Add New Part** to open the listing creation form. You can save your progress as a draft at any point before publishing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the Part Name and OEM Part Number">
    Type the standard part name (e.g., "Alternator," "Front Bumper Cover," "Transmission – Automatic") and enter the OEM part number printed on the part itself. If you have the interchange number as well, enter it in the designated field. Both numbers make your listing discoverable to more buyers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Donor Vehicle Information">
    Select the **year, make, model, and trim level** of the vehicle the part was removed from. Then enter the **donor vehicle's VIN** in the VIN field. AUAPW uses VIN data to auto-populate additional fitment details and verify compatibility.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the Condition Grade">
    Choose the grade that honestly reflects the part's condition: **Grade A**, **Grade B**, **Grade C**, or **Remanufactured**. See the Condition Grades section below for definitions. This field directly affects buyer expectations and return eligibility.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Part Mileage">
    Type in the mileage recorded on the donor vehicle at the time the part was pulled. If you don't know the exact mileage, enter your best estimate and note this in the listing description.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload Photos">
    Upload a minimum of **3 photos** and a maximum of **20 photos** per listing. Photos must meet the requirements outlined in the Photos section below. You can drag and drop files or browse from your device.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Your Price">
    Enter your asking price in USD. Review the [Pricing Guidelines](/sellers/pricing-guidelines) page for tips on setting competitive prices and understanding how AUAPW fees apply.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Dimensions and Weight">
    Enter the part's packaged length, width, height (in inches), and weight (in pounds). AUAPW uses these values to calculate shipping costs that are displayed to buyers at checkout. Inaccurate dimensions can result in shipping cost disputes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Publish">
    Review all fields for accuracy, then click **Publish Listing**. Your part will be live and searchable on AUAPW within a few minutes. You can return to edit the listing at any time from the Inventory section of your dashboard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Condition Grades

Condition grades are the primary signal buyers use to evaluate a part before purchasing. Assign the grade that most accurately reflects the part's actual state — never inflate the grade to justify a higher price.

| Grade | Definition | Typical Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| **Grade A** | Excellent condition. No visible damage, cracks, or heavy wear. Low mileage preferred. | Low-mileage pulls, late-model vehicles |
| **Grade B** | Good condition. Minor surface wear, small scuffs, or cosmetic imperfections that do not affect function. | Average-mileage pulls, mechanical parts in working order |
| **Grade C** | Acceptable condition. Noticeable wear, discoloration, or minor damage. Fully functional but cosmetically imperfect. | High-mileage pulls, older vehicles, non-visible parts |
| **Remanufactured** | The part has been professionally rebuilt or reconditioned to OEM specifications. Must include documentation of the remanufacturing process. | Alternators, starters, brake calipers, steering racks |

<Warning>
Listing a part at a higher condition grade than it deserves is one of the most common causes of buyer disputes and return requests. Inaccurate grading negatively impacts your seller rating and, if it happens repeatedly, can result in account suspension. When in doubt, grade down and explain the condition clearly in the description field.
</Warning>

## Photos

Strong photos are your most powerful selling tool. Buyers cannot inspect a part in person, so your images need to show everything they would want to see.

**Photo Requirements:**
- Minimum **3 photos**, maximum **20 photos** per listing
- Images must be at least **800 × 600 pixels**
- Accepted formats: **JPG, PNG, WEBP**
- Maximum file size: **10 MB per image**
- No watermarks, promotional overlays, or stock images

**Photo Tips:**

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Lighting" icon="sun" href="/sellers/listing-parts">
    Shoot in natural daylight or under bright, even artificial light. Avoid shadows that obscure detail. Dark or blurry photos are the single biggest reason buyers skip a listing.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Angles" icon="camera" href="/sellers/listing-parts">
    Include front, back, top, bottom, and both sides where applicable. Show any mounting points, connectors, or hardware that comes with the part.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Part Number Tag" icon="tag" href="/sellers/listing-parts">
    Always photograph the OEM part number tag or stamped number directly on the part. This gives buyers immediate confirmation that the part matches their need.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Damage Disclosure" icon="triangle-exclamation" href="/sellers/listing-parts">
    If the part has any cosmetic blemishes, scratches, or non-functional damage, photograph these clearly. Honest disclosure prevents returns and builds trust.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## OEM vs. Interchange Numbers

When buyers search AUAPW, they may search by the OEM number stamped on their broken part, by the interchange number from a cross-reference catalog, or by fitment (year/make/model). Entering both the OEM number and the interchange number on your listing ensures it surfaces in all three search paths.

- **OEM Part Number:** The number assigned by the original vehicle manufacturer. Found stamped or printed on the part itself or in OEM parts catalogs.
- **Interchange Number:** A standardized number (e.g., from Hollander or MOTOR interchange guides) that identifies a part as compatible across multiple vehicle applications.

<Info>
If you use a parts management system like Hollander or Car-Part Pro, interchange numbers are typically included in your existing inventory data. Copying them into your AUAPW listings requires no additional research.
</Info>

<Tip>
Including the donor vehicle's VIN in your listing builds significantly more buyer confidence than year/make/model data alone. A VIN confirms the exact trim level, engine, and option packages — eliminating the most common "will this fit my car?" questions before they're asked.
</Tip>
